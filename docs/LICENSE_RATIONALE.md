# License Rationale

This project is licensed under the **MIT License**.

## Why MIT?

When evaluating open-source licenses, our primary goal was to find the "most permissive" license possible to encourage widespread use, contribution, and minimal friction for users.

We compared several options, most notably the MIT License and the Apache 2.0 License:

*   **MIT License:** Extremely short, easy to read, and widely understood. It allows anyone to use, modify, distribute, and even sell the code in proprietary software, with the only requirement being to include the original copyright notice and license text.
*   **Apache 2.0 License:** Also highly permissive, but significantly longer and more complex ("written by lawyers, for lawyers"). Its primary advantage over MIT is an explicit patent grant and protection against patent trolls.

### Conclusion

For this project, we prioritized **simplicity and ease of adoption**. The MIT License achieves this perfectly by removing complex legal jargon while still providing the fundamental freedoms of open-source software. We do not currently have complex patent concerns that would necessitate the heavier Apache 2.0 license.

Therefore, the MIT License was chosen to keep the barrier to entry as low as possible and maximize the project's utility for the community.
